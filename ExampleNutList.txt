Plastic
Nylon 6/6— The most common plastic nuts, these are good for general purpose fastening. They are slippery and abrasion resistant, so they thread on smoothly and stand up to daily wear and tear. Nylon will swell and weaken when exposed to moisture, so these nuts are best for dry environments.
High-Strength Glass-Filled Nylon—Glass fibers embedded in the nylon give these nuts better strength than nylon 6/6. They can also handle higher temperatures without losing strength.
Moisture-Resistant Acetal—The best plastic for damp and wet environments, these nuts won't swell or lose their strength if exposed to moisture. They also resist wear and scratches better than nylon nuts.
Clear Impact-Resistant Polycarbonate—Our most impact-resistant plastic nuts, these withstand bumps and vibration without cracking or denting. They are also clear to be less noticeable in your assembly.
Acid-Resistant PVC—Connect sensors, fittings, and other equipment in chemical-processing lines. These nuts won't degrade when exposed to harsh acids, alkalis, and alcohols.
Chemical-Resistant Polypropylene—Versatile and economical, these resist a broad range of chemicals, such as petroleum, degreasers, and sulfuric acid. They're not quite as chemical resistant as PTFE nuts and will weaken if exposed to chlorine, lacquer solvents, or nitric acid.
High-Temperature Chemical-Resistant PTFE—The most chemical-resistant plastic nuts we offer, these stand up to the widest range of harsh acids, bases, solvents, and corrosive cleaners. They also keep their shape and strength at higher temperatures than most plastics.
High-Strength High-Temperature PEEK—A close alternative to metal nuts, these offer the best strength of all our plastic nuts to stand up to high-stress applications. They also won't degrade when exposed to high heat, so they are a good choice for joining components on circuit boards and other electrical applications. They aren't very impact resistant, however, and may crack or dent if subjected to hard bumps and vibrations.
Noncontaminating PVDF—The highest purity nuts we offer, these won't release contaminants into your process line that can damage critical components. They are commonly used in clean rooms for pharmaceutical and semiconductor manufacturing, as well as in food and beverage applications. PVDF is comparable to Kynar and Solef.
